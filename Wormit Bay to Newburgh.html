Wormit Bay to Newburgh

The Kincardine to Limekilns section is a curious blend of modern industry, cultural heritage, nature reserves and iconic bridges. The path stretches for 11 miles or 17.5km. When following the Coastal Path keep an eye out for the 'Fife Costal Path' waymarker on various road and cycle paths, because it's kinda confusing to stay on the right track. Begin at Kincardine Bridge and head east following cycle path route 76.

The path joins up with the railway line as you near the historic village of Culross. From here you skirt the edge of Torry Bay Local Nature Reserve with its rare salt marsh plant communities. Continue on to Limekilns and Charlestown> Be sure to take a detour to the old kilns if you have time, or stroll along the promenade to the quaint harbour.

Allow 4 - 5 hours to complete the whole route.
